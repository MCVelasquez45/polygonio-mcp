Selling Premium = Being the House

A credit spread collects premium upfront and profits when the market does nothing dramatic. The House Strategy sells options with ~20% chance of expiring in the money — ~80% of the time, you keep the premium.

Risk-On vs Risk-Off: Reading the Room

How sector dashboards tell you which direction to trade
Intuition ▸
Concept
Don't Guess the Trend — Read the Dashboard

When offensive sectors (tech, consumer discretionary) are green and mega-caps are trending up, it's risk-on — sell put spreads. When defensive sectors lead, it's risk-off — sell call spreads. Mixed signals? Don't trade.

→ From strategy: entry_only_if_clear_risk_on_or_off: true & avoid_mixed_dashboard_signals: true
1

Check sector ETFs: XLK, XLY (offensive) vs XLU, XLV (defensive). Which group is green? This tells you whether institutions are buying risk or hiding from it.

2

Check mega-cap leaders: NVDA, AAPL, MSFT, AMZN, GOOGL. Trending together? These drive ~30% of SPX. If they're all up, SPX is likely going up.

3

Mixed signals? No trade today. The hardest skill in trading is doing nothing.

4

Clear risk-on? Put spread below. Risk-off? Call spread above. Trade WITH the trend. The market is already doing the work.

What Happens After You Enter

Exit rules, Vega risk, and the last 15 minutes
Discipline ▸
Concept
Theta Is Your Friend, Vega Is Your Enemy

Time decay (theta) erodes option value — money flowing to you every minute. But if implied volatility spikes (vega), option prices jump even without price reaching your strike. The final 15 minutes are where gamma risk peaks — small moves become giant P&L swings.

1

After entry, monitor on 15-min chart. If price respects S/R levels, do nothing. Default action is hold. Theta is working for you.

2

Price approaching strike? Don't panic. Mark-to-market losses can reverse. A touch isn't a breach.

3

After 15:45, the strike becomes "true" — small moves cause real losses. Gamma explodes near expiry. A 2-point SPX move can flip a winner to a loss.

4

Unexpected news spikes IV? Option prices rise even without price threatening your strike. Vega risk — the silent killer. News events are the #1 cause.

✓ Uses existing: PaperTradingDashboard (real-time P&L, position monitoring, pause/resume) + WebSocket streams (futures:market:update, position:update)

What Can Go Wrong (And What It Costs)

Stress test scenarios — experience loss before risking real money
Discipline ▸
Concept
The 80/20 Trap

This strategy wins ~80% of the time. But a single loss can wipe out 3-4 wins. $5-wide spreads collecting $1.00 premium: a full loss is $4.00. That's 4 perfect wins erased. If you don't feel this before real money, you'll panic and break your rules.

1

Scenario: Fed surprise. VIX 14 to 22. Your 20-delta spread is now 45-delta. Down $800 on a $200 expected gain. This is why avoid_news_days exists. But not all news is predictable.

2

Scenario: Late-day sell-off. SPX drops 30 points in 20 minutes. Your strike is now ITM with 10 min left. This is why the strategy considers the strike "true" after 15:45.

3

Scenario: Three losses in a row. Each loss is 4x a win. You've given back 12 winning days. Can you trade Monday? Professionals expect this. Amateurs quit here.

✓ Uses existing: Stress Test Framework (BacktestResultsPanel runs scenarios with parameter overrides, compares vs baseline) + Promotion Gate (won't allow deployment until checks pass)
Hands-On Task

Run the stress test suite on the House Strategy with $10,000 capital. Read the scenarios where the strategy breaks down. Then check if the Promotion Gate would pass this strategy — what score does it get?
